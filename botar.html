Oliver Botar

Sensory Derangement:
Dis/Re-orientation and the European Avant-Garde

A public lecture, in conjunction with FAH347

Tuesday, March 21, 5 - 7PM, Sidney Smith 1085

Oliver A. I. Botar is Professor of Art History at the University of Manitoba

His research focuses on early 20th century Central European Modernism (particularly the work of László Moholy-Nagy), with concentrations on art in alternative (“new”) media, and “Biocentrism” and Modernism in early-to-mid 20th-century art. He also specializes in modern Hungarian and Canadian art in general, and has lectured extensively in Europe, North America and Japan.
