Instructions

Below are three separate question groupings, for short, medium, and long answer questions. Answer one question from each group (just to be clear, you'll answer a total of three questions, each of different length).

This exam isn't designed to take a full week, however, I'm setting aside a full week for you to produce your best work, including time for editing, reviewing/finding texts, and to take your time.

Because this is an open-book exam, you are expected to actively reference and cite scholarship in your answers. References need not be limited to course readings, you are welcome, encouraged even, to use additional sources (that is, scholarly articles, books, or chapters that are directly relevant to the exam questions).

The best answers will demonstrate a strong understanding of course material by...

  • Clearly outlining the historical, cultural, and theoretical context of the topic (the "what happened and why does it matter?" of it).
  • Using appropriate scholarly texts, in appropriate places, to support your explanations, and to reference other interpretations of it (the "who has said what about it").
  • Balancing explanation and scholarly references with your own analysis and interpretation (the "this is what I think of all this" aspect of it).
